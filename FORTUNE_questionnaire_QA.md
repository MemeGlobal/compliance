## 8. Data Security

**Question**

Data Security. Please describe your data security practices and how you ensure the security and confidentiality of Personal Information provided to you, and protect such information against unauthorized access, use, destruction and loss. Do you encrypt Personal Information and if so, at what times and under what circumstances?

**Answer**

Trinity has implemented appropriate technical and organizational measures to safeguard data processed by Trinity. These include, among others:

- Restrict access to data to only those personnel that require such access consistent with the concepts of least privilege and need-to-know, and segregation of duties.
- Maintain appropriate network security measures, including but not limited to firewalls and intrusion prevention systems.
- Perform periodic vulnerability scanning and remediate identified vulnerabilities in a timely, risk-based manner, including timely implementation of all manufacturers and developer recommended security updates and patches to operating systems and third-party software.
- Penetration testing is conducted on a periodic basis.
- Antivirus and malware protection software are implemented with up-to-date definitions.
- Complex password requirements are enforced.
- Remote network and system access to the database are protected by multi-factor authentication.
- Authentication credentials stored on applicable systems are encrypted or hashed.
- Secure workstation protection policies, including locking the system after a defined number of incorrect authentication attempts and password-protected screensavers that are activated after a defined period of inactivity.

Where applicable, personal data is encrypted in transit. IP is encrypted in rest. Access logs are stored for applicable systems.
